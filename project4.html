Project 4: Open-source Assistive Technology

Review Documentation

We first registered for the Enable hub and reviewed the project. My partner and I originally went for a different print than the Phoenix Hand V3, but that print ended up being too complicated. The directions were in Spanish, and there were additional pieces required that our kit didnâ€™t have. In the end, we decided to go with the Phoenix Hand V3 and began the printing process.

Print and Assemble the Model

Once we finalized the design, we began the printing process. It took several hours for the parts to be printed. Afterward, we assembled the model according to the instructions, ensuring that each component fit correctly. Despite some minor challenges with aligning the pieces, the final assembly was successful, and the hand mechanism functioned as intended.

Extend, Critique & Reflect

This project provided a unique opportunity to explore open-source assistive technology. While the final model worked well, there were aspects of the process that could be improved. The initial documentation was unclear, and switching to the Phoenix Hand V3 simplified the process significantly. Future improvements could include more detailed assembly instructions and additional support for first-time users. Overall, this project has broadened our understanding of assistive devices and how open-source projects can contribute to accessibility solutions.
