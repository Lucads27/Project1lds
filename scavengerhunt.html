Scavenger Hunt

Designs for Access

1. Evidence of design for navigation without one or more of the 5 senses

The photo above is of Braille in front of a door, allowing visually impaired people to navigate through a building.

2. Evidence of design for forms of assistance, broadly defined

This photo shows a wheelchair lift up stairs, which assists people who have lower limb mobility issues.

3. Evidence of design for aging, broadly defined

Here we have railing, which helps aging people as, when you age, you lose your balance.

4. Evidence of design for young children (no parks or playgrounds)

Here we have two water fountains; one is designed for children as it is short and close to the ground.

5. Evidence of design for wheeled mobile gear (chairs, bikes, strollers, etc.)

This photo is of a bike stand, allowing people to park their bikes.

Designs against Access

6. Evidence of design with physical barriers

Here we have a staircase with no ramp or access for people who use wheelchairs.

7. Evidence of design with a lack of information

Here we have something outside of the engineering classroom. Unfortunately, I don't know what it was designed for; however, it was near the elevator and had no labels or explanation.

8. Evidence of design with an absence of help

While I know this is a fire extinguisher, there are no clear directions on how to use it. While it may be intuitive, the usage should be more clearly labeled and have larger images.

Design Priorities in the Built Environment

9. Evidence of design for historical specificity

These are antique pots and bowls made from clay, used for carrying water and foods.

10. Evidence of design for environmental sustainability

Here we have a digital projector. Instead of using markers, erasers, or chalk, teachers can use this to be more environmentally sustainable, as projectors create far less waste.

11. Evidence of design for health and well-being

Here we have an eye wash station outside of a science classroom. This ensures the health and well-being of students and faculty in emergency situations.

12. Evidence of design for transportation

Here we have a Ninebot scooter, which is designed for transportation. It is electric and allows for easy, eco-friendly transportation.
